Classic Homemade Lasagna

This traditional Italian lasagna recipe features layers of rich meat sauce, creamy cheese, and perfectly cooked pasta. It's the ultimate comfort food that brings families together around the dinner table. This recipe has been passed down through generations and creates a hearty, satisfying meal that's perfect for special occasions or Sunday dinners.

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour 15 minutes

Servings: 8 portions

Difficulty Level: Intermediate

Fresh ingredients ready for assembly

Ingredients List

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 blend)
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions to follow

Please Follow these instructions to the leter

  1. Prepare the pasta: Cook lasagna noodles according to package directions until al dente. Drain and set aside on a clean kitchen towel to prevent sticking.
  2. Make the meat sauce: In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium-high heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, cooking until onion is translucent. Drain excess fat and stir in marinara sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Prepare cheese mixture: In a medium bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, fresh basil, and half of the mozzarella cheese. Mix well until smooth and creamy.
  4. Preheat and prep: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with cooking spray or butter.
  5. Layer the lasagna: Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of the baking dish. Layer with 4 noodles, half of the ricotta mixture, one-third of remaining meat sauce, and sprinkle with mozzarella. Repeat layers once more.
  6. Final layer: Top with remaining 4 noodles, remaining meat sauce, remaining mozzarella, and all of the Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake: Cover tightly with foil and bake for 35 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 10 minutes until cheese is golden and bubbly.
  8. Rest and serve: Let lasagna cool for 10 minutes before cutting to allow layers to set properly. Serve hot with garlic bread and a fresh salad.

Tips & Notes

Pro Tips: For the best results, use fresh herbs when possible, and don't skip the resting time after baking - it helps the layers hold together when serving. You can assemble this lasagna up to 24 hours ahead of time and refrigerate before baking. If cooking from cold, add an extra 15-20 minutes to the baking time. Freeze leftovers for up to 3 months for easy weeknight dinners!
